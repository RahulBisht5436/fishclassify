Striped Red Mullet

The striped red mullet, also known as Mullus surmuletus, is a species of fish that belongs to the family Mullidae. It is commonly found in the coastal waters of the eastern Atlantic Ocean, including the Mediterranean Sea, the Black Sea, and parts of the North Atlantic Ocean. The striped red mullet is known for its distinctive appearance, with vivid red and silver stripes on its body, and its delicate, flavorful flesh. The striped red mullet has a slender body with a laterally compressed shape, and typically grows to a length of 15-30 centimeters (6-12 inches). It has a small, pointed head with large eyes and a protruding lower jaw. The body is covered with small, smooth scales, and the coloration of the fish can vary depending on its age, location, and habitat. The most notable feature of the striped red mullet is its distinctive color pattern, with alternating red and silver vertical stripes along its body, which can vary in intensity.

The striped red mullet is a benthic species, which means it lives near the bottom of the sea floor, often in rocky or sandy areas. It is a carnivorous fish that feeds on small invertebrates, such as crustaceans, mollusks, and worms. In culinary terms, striped red mullet is highly prized for its delicate, tender flesh and distinctive flavor. Its flesh is white, firm, and moist, with a slightly sweet, nutty taste. It is often considered a delicacy in Mediterranean cuisine and is used in a variety of preparations, including grilling, frying, baking, and broiling. It can also be used in soups, stews, and risottos.
